Nutrition: 72 Hour Fast

January 3, 2021

I recently fasted for 3 days, and it was really hard. From 7:40 PM on 12/18/20 through 7:40 PM on Monday, 12/21/20, I did not eat anything. The only nutrients I consumed were water, black coffee, salt, and lemon juice. This post is a log of my thoughts and feelings I had throughout the 3 days.

  • 12/18/20 at 7:40 PM: I finished my last meal, which was notably carb heavy. I ate a lot of carbs in pasta and only a bit of protein in small portions of salmon and chicken.

  • 12/19/20 at 8:50 AM: I wrote in my journal, “The fast is going well so far… 13 hours in, 59 to go!”

  • 12/20/20 at 12:10 AM: I wrote, “This fast is tough. Jeez.” This was interesting because I have done several 36 hour fasts and one 48 hour fast before, and this was definitely the worst I had felt in any of them this early into the fast (28.5 hours).

  • 12/20/20 at 9:46 AM: After a very difficult morning wakeup during which I felt dizzy, lethargic, and almost broke my fast, I read online that I was probably lacking electrolytes and should try squeezing lemon juice and putting a pinch of salt into my water. I did so, and wrote, “I’m feeling better after some salt and lemon in my water. Lesson: don’t eat a carb-heavy meal before going on an extended fast. I felt a high heart rate and weakness this morning, but I’m feeling better now.”

  • 12/20/20 at 3:01 PM: I wrote, “Significantly better mood for the past 3 hours. Coffee probably helped. Feel very alert and present. Productive. Night and day difference relative to when I wanted to break the fast this morning.

  • 12/20/20 at 9:49 PM: “Definitely feel slight weakness in my legs. Would definitely like food right now, but less than 24 hours to go!”

  • 12/20/20 at 11:21 PM: “This fast has been a rollercoaster. Thankfully the finish line is near. After a stretch from 11 AM - 6 PM of feeling good (drinking coffee helped), I started feeling weak, tired, and unmotivated.”

  • 12/21/20 at 9:53 AM: “10 hours left!! You can do it.” I had trouble falling sleeping that night, but the quality of sleep was good once I did.

  • 12/21/20 at 12:38 PM: “I am hungry.”

  • 12/21/20 at 7:40 PM: I break my fast with a small salad, a couple brats, and some sweet potatoes.

  • 12/21/20 at 11:53 PM: “The fast ended. I’m not sure I’ll ever do 72 hours again. Fortunately, though, the last few hours weren’t too bad. I was just very ready to eat. I don’t think my mood was great throughout. I definitely felt more irritable than I would’ve liked, but today I got everything done I had planned. I felt more focused and motivated today.”

I definitely took several things away from this fast. On one hand, I think having a carb-heavy meal before the fast threw my body off and caused a lot of the problems I experienced. As I said, in my past 24 and 36 hour fasts and one 48 hour fast, I never felt as bad as I did as soon as I did in this one. On another, in my best fasting moments I feel more alert, productive, and focused than I do when I’m not fasting. I’ve also found that, within 24 hours of beginning my fasts, exercise is no problem and I feel just as strong as I do normally.

As for why I fast… well, I was first intrigued on a trip to Morocco in the Spring of 2019, when my Moroccan “host-dad” told me that I should try fasting for a day and see how I felt. I also found the importance of fasting to Islam quite interesting. I finally tried my first fast (24 hours) in early 2020, had a good experience, and have since done more 24 hour fasts, a few 36 hour fasts, a 48 hour fast, and now a 72 hour fast. I believe fasting has helped me be more conscious of what, when, and how I eat my food. For example, now I’m more conscious that I don’t have to eat just because it’s breakfast, lunch, or dinner time. Rather, I should have the discipline to eat when I’m hungry. I also am better about eating until I’m full as opposed to eating a lot just because I can. Further, people today undoubtedly eat far more often than they need to. Most science suggests that humans can survive AT LEAST 30 days without food. Yet, many people I’ve encountered cannot go 3 hours without eating a snack. Overall, fasting has helped me improve my discipline as it relates to both food and life in general, and I plan to do more in the future. Whether I do another 72 hour fast remains to be seen, but shorter ones (24-48 hours) are definitely in my future.
